## Section 1: Concept Check - Answers

### Question 1: Avoiding Jargon

**Why important:**
- Users may not understand and feel confused/frustrated
- Miscommunication leads to incorrect assumptions
- Users may say "yes" without understanding
- Creates barrier between IT and business
- User may feel talked down to or embarrassed

**Better approach:** Use analogies, explain in terms they understand, check for understanding

---

### Question 2: Handling Angry Customers

1. **Stay calm** - Don't match their energy
2. **Acknowledge their frustration** - "I understand this is frustrating"
3. **Don't take it personally** - They're frustrated at the situation
4. **Listen actively** - Let them vent without interrupting
5. **Focus on solution** - Move conversation toward fixing the problem
6. **Apologize for the inconvenience** (not blame)
7. **Set expectations** - What you'll do and when
8. **Escalate if needed** - If they remain hostile

---

### Question 3: Setting Expectations

**Why important:**
- Users can plan their work around the timeline
- Reduces follow-up calls asking for status
- Builds trust through reliability
- Prevents disappointment from unrealistic hopes
- Creates accountability for support team

**Key things to communicate:** Timeline, what will happen, what user needs to do, how you'll follow up

---

### Question 4: Active Listening

**What it is:** Fully concentrating on what's being said rather than passively hearing

**Components:**
- Not interrupting
- Paraphrasing to confirm understanding
- Asking clarifying questions
- Noting non-verbal cues (tone, frustration)
- Summarizing the issue back

**Why important for troubleshooting:**
- Catches details the user might not think are important
- Prevents assumptions that lead to wrong solutions
- Shows user you care about their issue
- Builds rapport and trust

---

### Question 5: Post-Ticket Documentation

**Should document:**
- Problem description (user's words and technical)
- Troubleshooting steps taken
- Resolution or current status
- Root cause (if determined)
- Time spent
- Any follow-up needed
- Related tickets/known issues
- User feedback/confirmation

## Section 2: Activities - Answers

### Activity A: Technical Translation

| Technical | User-Friendly |
|-----------|---------------|
| "DNS server isn't responding" | "Your computer is having trouble finding website addresses. It's like having a phone book that's not available right now." |
| "Clear your browser cache" | "We need to delete the temporary files your browser saved. This is like cleaning out old notes to make room for new ones." |
| "DHCP gave you an APIPA address" | "Your computer couldn't get a proper network address from the server, so it gave itself a temporary one that doesn't work for internet access." |
| "Low on RAM and paging to disk" | "Your computer is running out of quick-access memory, so it's using the slower hard drive as extra space. This is why things feel slow." |
| "Driver conflict causing BSOD" | "Two pieces of software that help your hardware work are interfering with each other, causing the blue screen crash." |
| "SSD nearly full" | "Your storage is almost completely full, which is slowing down your computer. It's like trying to work at a desk covered in papers." |

### Activity B: Setting Expectations

**Scenario 1 (Password reset while busy):**
"I'm currently helping another user with an urgent issue, but I should be able to get to your password reset in about 15-20 minutes. Would you like me to call you back, or would you prefer to use the self-service portal? I can send you the link."

**Scenario 2 (Installation with restart):**
"This installation will require a restart that typically takes about 15 minutes. You won't be able to use your computer during that time. Is now a good time, or would you prefer to schedule this for your lunch break or end of day?"

**Scenario 3 (Escalation needed):**
"This issue requires our network team to investigate further. I'm going to escalate this to them now with all the information we've gathered. You should expect a response within 24-48 hours. I'll include your contact information so they can reach you directly. Is there a best time to contact you?"

**Scenario 4 (Need to research):**
"I want to make sure we get this right. I need to do some research to find the best solution. Can I call you back in about 30 minutes? I'll have a clearer picture of what we're dealing with by then."

### Activity C: Difficult Customers

**Scenario 1 (30-minute wait):**
"I sincerely apologize for the wait - I know your time is valuable. Let me help you right away. What's the issue you're experiencing?" 
(Acknowledge, apologize, move to action)

**Scenario 2 ("IT is useless"):**
"I'm sorry you've had frustrating experiences. I'm here to help resolve this issue for you. Can you tell me what's happening?"
(Don't be defensive, redirect to solving their problem)

**Scenario 3 ("Just fix it"):**
"Absolutely, I'll get this resolved for you. I may need to ask a few quick questions to fix it as fast as possible - is that okay?"
(Respect their time while getting what you need)

**Scenario 4 (Bypass security):**
"I understand this policy is inconvenient, but it's in place to protect company data and your account. I can't bypass it, but I can help you work within the process as quickly as possible. Let me see if there's a faster approved way to accomplish what you need."
(Firm but helpful, explain why, offer alternatives)

**Scenario 5 (Blamed personally):**
"I understand you're frustrated with this outage - it's impacting everyone's work. Let me focus on getting you back up and running. Here's what we're doing to resolve it..."
(Don't take the bait, redirect to solution)

### Activity D: Active Listening

**Techniques:**

| Technique | Example | Purpose |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| Paraphrasing | "So the slowness is mainly with Excel..." | Confirm understanding |
| Clarifying questions | "When did this start exactly?" | Get specific details |
| Summarizing | "Let me make sure I have this right..." | Verify complete picture |
| Non-verbal | Nodding, "I see," "Mm-hmm" | Show engagement |
| Not interrupting | Wait for them to finish | Get full story |

**For the given statement:**

**Paraphrase:** "So your computer has been slow, especially with Excel, sometimes freezing completely, and this started after last week's update."

**Clarifying questions:**
1. "Which update are you referring to - was it a Windows update or an Excel update?"
2. "When it freezes, do you have to hold the power button, or can you use Ctrl+Alt+Delete?"

**Summary:** "Let me make sure I understand: Since last week's update, you've been experiencing slowness mainly with Excel, and occasional complete freezes requiring a restart. Is that right?"

## Section 3: Reflection - Answers

### Scenario 1: Seeing Personal Document

**What to do:**
1. **Don't read it** - Look away immediately
2. **Don't mention it** - Not relevant to your work
3. **Don't tell others** - Maintain confidentiality
4. **Focus on your task** - Complete the support request
5. **If truly concerning** (illegal activity, threat): Report to manager or HR, not the user

---

### Scenario 2: Monitoring Software Request

**What to do:**
1. **Don't install it** - This could be unauthorized surveillance
2. **Check with IT management** - Verify if this is approved policy
3. **Document the request** - Protect yourself
4. **Refer to HR policies** - Employee monitoring has legal requirements
5. **Escalate to your manager** - Let them handle the conversation

**Important:** Even if a manager requests it, installing unauthorized monitoring software could violate employee rights and company policy.

---

### Scenario 3: Made Problem Worse

**How to handle:**
1. **Be honest** - Tell the user what happened
2. **Apologize** - "I made an error that's affected your issue..."
3. **Explain what you'll do** - Fix your mistake, resolve original issue
4. **Don't hide it** - Deception damages trust more than the mistake
5. **Learn from it** - Document to avoid repeating

**Example:** "I need to let you know that while troubleshooting, I made a change that has actually made things worse. I'm sorry about that. Here's what I'm going to do to fix it..."

---

### Scenario 4: VIP Interrupts Current Support

**How to handle:**
1. **Don't drop current user** - That's unprofessional
2. **Acknowledge VIP** - "I'll be with you in just a moment"
3. **Assess urgency** - Is VIP issue truly more urgent?
4. **If current task is quick** - Finish it, then help VIP
5. **If both urgent** - Get help from colleague for one
6. **Communicate with both** - Set expectations

**Example to VIP:** "I'm finishing up with another user and will be with you in about 5 minutes. Is your issue something I can have a colleague start on immediately?"

**Never:** Rudely dismiss one user for another based solely on title.
